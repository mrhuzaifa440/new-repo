Faisal Masjid

Located in Islamabad, Faisal Masjid is not only an impressive religious site but also a prominent tourist attraction. The mosque, named after King Faisal bin Abdulaziz of Saudi Arabia, was completed in 1986 and is considered one of the largest mosques in the world. With its stunning architecture and peaceful ambience, Faisal Masjid draws visitors from all over the globe. The mosque plays a significant role in the administration of the city, as it is not only a place of worship but also serves as a hub for various religious and cultural activities.

The construction of Faisal Masjid is a testament to the vision and dedication of Pakistan's administration. The mosque's design is a blend of traditional Islamic and modern architectural styles, featuring striking geometric patterns and intricate calligraphy. The main prayer hall can accommodate up to 100,000 worshippers, and the surrounding courtyard can hold even more. The mosque also houses a library, conference hall, and other facilities to cater to the needs of the community and visitors. With its grandeur and rich cultural contents, Faisal Masjid is a must-visit landmark in Islamabad.

Governing Bodies and Administrative Setup

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, is governed by multiple bodies and administrative setups. The city is under the jurisdiction of the Islamabad Capital Territory (ICT), which is headed by the Islamabad Capital Territory Administration (ICTA). The ICTA oversees various departments responsible for the smooth functioning of the city, including health, education, transport, and sports. The Capital Development Authority (CDA) is another key governing body responsible for planning, development, and maintenance of the city's infrastructure, while the Islamabad Metropolitan Corporation (IMC) focuses on local governance. These governing bodies work in conjunction to ensure proper administration and provision of essential services to the residents of Islamabad.
