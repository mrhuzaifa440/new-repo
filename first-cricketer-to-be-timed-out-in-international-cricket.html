FAQ

What is a timed out dismissal in international cricket?

A timed out dismissal occurs when a batsman takes too long to take his position at the crease after a dismissal of his teammate. He must be ready to face the next delivery within three minutes, failing which he can be timed out.

Who was the first cricketer to be timed out in international cricket?

Bill Brown, an Australian cricketer, was the first player to be timed out in international cricket. It happened during a Test match against England in 1951.

What led to Bill Brown's timed out dismissal?

Bill Brown's dismissal was a result of a misunderstanding between him and his batting partner. He was attempting to change his gloves but his partner, Neil Harvey, assumed he was ready and walked out to the crease, leading to Brown's timed out dismissal.

How did the umpires make the decision for a timed out dismissal?

The umpires are responsible for monitoring the time taken by the dismissed batsman to reach the crease. If they determine that the batsman has taken longer than three minutes, they can give him a timed out dismissal.

Are timed out dismissals common in international cricket?

No, timed out dismissals are extremely rare in international cricket. Bill Brown's case remains the only instance of a timed out dismissal in Test cricket, and there have been only a few occurrences in limited-overs matches.

How does a timed out dismissal compare to other uncommon modes of dismissal in cricket?

Timed out dismissals are among the least common modes of dismissal in cricket. They are much rarer compared to more familiar forms of dismissals such as catches, bowled, or run-outs.

What are the implications of a timed out dismissal for the cricketer and the team?

A timed out dismissal can have various consequences for the cricketer and the team. The batsman who is timed out is considered to be out without scoring any runs, and it can lead to a loss of momentum for the batting team.

How did the first timed out dismissal shape the future of international cricket?

The first timed out dismissal brought attention to the rules and regulations regarding this form of dismissal. It prompted discussions and potential revisions to ensure fair play and reduce misunderstandings between batsmen.

Can you provide examples of timed out dismissals in subsequent international matches?
