The Historical Background of Islamabad

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, has a rich historical background. It was chosen as the capital in 1960, replacing Karachi. The decision to create a new capital was influenced by the need for a centrally located city and the desire to have a purpose-built capital. The construction of Islamabad began in the 1960s under the guidance of architectural firm Doxiadis Associates. However, due to misinformation regarding the location, arrests were made, and the project faced several setbacks. Despite these challenges, the development of Islamabad continued, and today it stands as a symbol of modernity and progress in Pakistan.

The historical background of Islamabad also includes the transformation of the area from a barren and undeveloped land to a vibrant and planned city.
