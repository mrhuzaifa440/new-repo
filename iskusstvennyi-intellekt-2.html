However, with this newfound power, AI has become like a bicycle that we can no longer control. Initially, AI algorithms were trained on a limited amount of images, resulting in bias. To overcome this, researchers have introduced more diverse images to ensure fairness and accuracy. The advancement of AI technology faces not only technical obstacles but also ethical challenges. For instance, when discussing autonomous cars, AI analyzes sensor data collected during real-world driving to make informed decisions, distinguishing it from any other driver. This capability is made possible by algorithms known as autonomous driving programs. These algorithms learn from analyzing thousands of hours of video footage of ideal driving to make instant decisions on the road. AI has also played a significant role in fields such as facial recognition, where it can calculate unique proportions for each face, a process that was previously nonexistent. Moreover, AI has made significant contributions in healthcare, detecting early-stage malignant tumors and avoiding collisions in space exploration. The future of AI looks promising, whether it be in driverless vehicles or predicting weather forecasts. By identifying patterns in data, we can correct biases in our training data, improving the overall reliability of AI systems. AI can also recreate spoken phrases in textual form, generate predictions, and create new sentences. The power of AI is undeniable, and its impact will only continue to grow in the future.

Artificial intelligence has evolved from its early stages of rule-based systems to more advanced machine learning and deep learning algorithms, allowing machines to learn and improve their performance over time.

Key technologies of artificial intelligence include machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, robotics, and neural networks, among others.

Artificial intelligence finds applications in various fields such as healthcare, finance, manufacturing, customer service, transportation, cybersecurity, and entertainment, among others.
